Poke Bowl: A Fresh and Flavorful Hawaiian Delight

A poke bowl is a Hawaiian dish that features cubed raw fish (usually tuna or salmon) served over a bed of rice and topped with a variety of fresh ingredients such as avocado, cucumber, seaweed, edamame, mango, and sesame seeds. Itâ€™s often dressed with soy sauce, spicy mayo, or other flavorful sauces, combining elements of Japanese and Hawaiian cuisine for a light, customizable, and nutritious meal.

Ingredients

  • Sushi rice or brown rice
  • Fresh raw fish (usually tuna or salmon)
  • Avocado
  • Cucumber
  • Edamame
  • Seaweed salad
  • Mango
  • Green onions
  • Sesame seeds
  • Pickled ginger
  • Soy sauce
  • Spicy mayo or sriracha mayo
  • Ponzu or sesame dressing
  • Nori (seaweed strips)
  1. Cook sushi rice (or brown rice) and let it cool slightly.
  2. Cut fresh raw fish (like tuna or salmon) into small cubes and toss the fish with soy sauce, sesame oil, and a little green onion or sesame seeds for flavor.
  3. Slice or dice vegetables and fruits such as cucumber, avocado, mango, and radish.
  4. Place rice in the bottom of a bowl and add the marinated fish on top.
  5. Drizzle with spicy mayo, ponzu, or sesame dressing and sprinkle sesame seeds, green onions, or seaweed strips on top.
  6. Mix everything together just before eating for the best flavor and texture.
